Courtrooms in Lviv

How typical courtroom looks like

In Ukraine most courtrooms are standardized (view from the spectators’ perspective):

– Judges sit against the wall on the podium with the National Emblem of Ukraine above them. The National Flag can be placed to the right or left of the podium;

– Close to the judges (can be on the podium or otherwise) – a Court Secretary, who takes care of recording of the court proceedings;

– To the right of the podium there is usually the Defence party – Defendant and Defence Counsel (if defendant is in custody, then the defendant’s closed facility is usually behind the defence counsel, so that they can communicate throughout the court hearing).

– To the left of the podium there is the Prosecution Party (Public Prosecutor, Victim, Counsel for the victim);

– The location of witness boxes can vary, but usually they are placed in such a position that the witness stands with his/her face to the judges and parties, and with his/her back to the spectators.

– On the tables for the parties and in the witness box there are usually microphones to record all the court hearings.

However, the court layout and courtrooms can differ substantially.
